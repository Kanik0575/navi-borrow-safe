## How I found them

Found people mostly through BITS alumni groups and personal network — asked if anyone had been rejected for a personal loan recently or was struggling with EMIs. More people said yes than I expected. I was upfront that it was for a student project, not a sales call. Most conversations were 45–60 minutes over Google Meet or in person. A couple got a Swiggy voucher as thanks.

---

## Key Demographic Observations

- **Age range:** 22–34 (all within Navi's primary digital lending demographic)
- **Income range:** ₹22k–₹67k/month (covers fair to good credit eligibility boundary)
- **Employment split:** 6 salaried, 1 self-employed (P5), 1 freelancer (P6's partner, mentioned in session)
- **Apps tested:** Navi appeared in 5 of 8 sessions — highest recall among digital NBFCs
- **Rejection rate in sample:** 6/8 (75%) — slightly above published industry average, consistent with self-selection toward users who had "something to talk about"

---

## Notes on the data

- No recordings — I took notes during each conversation and wrote up a summary immediately after
- Everyone knew it was for a student project and gave permission for anonymised use
- The income and situation details are self-reported; I didn't verify bank statements or anything like that
