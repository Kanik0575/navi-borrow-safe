# Interview Guide — Navi Borrow-Safe

**Kanik Kumar · March–April 2026**  
Questions I used across 8 conversations about the personal loan rejection experience. Google Meet or in-person, usually 45–60 minutes. I took notes during each session — no recordings.

---

## Who I was looking for

People who'd applied for a digital personal loan in the last year — either got rejected (at least once) or got approved but found the EMI harder than they expected. Found them through alumni groups and personal network. Tried to get a mix of rejected and approved people so I wasn't just hearing one side.

---

## How I opened each conversation

*"Hey, thanks for making time. I'm a student at BITS doing a project on the personal loan experience — specifically what happens when people get rejected. I'm not from any bank or lender. Whatever you share stays between us, I'll only use anonymous themes. No right or wrong answers — I just want to understand what actually happened for you. Cool if I take notes?"*

---

## Section 1: Context (5 min)

**Goal:** Understand their financial situation and relationship with credit without priming them on our hypotheses.

1. Walk me through the last time you needed extra money. What was the situation?
2. What did you consider — personal loan, credit card advance, borrowing from family? Why did you choose what you chose?
3. Have you ever applied for a personal loan through an app? Which one(s)?

---

## Section 2: The Application Experience (10 min)

**Goal:** Understand the application process UX — friction, trust, form completion.

4. Tell me what happened from the moment you decided to apply to when you got a response.
5. How long did the form take? Did anything confuse you or make you stop?
6. What did you expect to happen after you submitted?
7. *(If approved):* What did the approval screen look like? Did you feel you understood what you were agreeing to?

---

## Section 3: The Rejection Moment (15 min — only for rejected participants)

**Goal:** Capture the emotional and informational experience of rejection. DO NOT use the word "FOIR" at any point.

8. Tell me exactly what you saw when your application was declined. What did the screen say?
9. How did that make you feel?
10. Did you understand *why* you were rejected? In your own words, what did you think the reason was?
11. *(If they say they didn't know):* What would you have needed to see to understand the reason?
12. What did you do immediately after? Did you try another app? Did you give up?
13. Has the lender ever communicated with you after the rejection — email, SMS, push notification?
14. If you had been told exactly why you were rejected and given a specific 3-step plan to fix it, would that have changed what you did next?

---

## Section 4: Financial Literacy & Affordability (10 min)

**Goal:** Understand whether users self-assess affordability, and how well they understand their own debt position.

15. When you decided how much to apply for, how did you choose that amount?
16. Did you calculate what the monthly payment would be before applying?
17. *(If approved and struggling):* Looking back, what did you know about your income vs. your loan payment before you took the loan? Were you warned about anything?
18. Have you ever heard the term "FOIR" or "debt-to-income ratio"? *(If no):* I'll explain what it means — [explain in plain language]. Does that help you understand your rejection now?

---

## Section 5: Reactions to Concepts (10 min)

**Goal:** Validate the product concepts without leading the witness.

19. *(Show the diagnosis concept on phone — actual app screen):* If you'd seen this after your rejection, what would you have thought? Would you have trusted it?
20. *(Show the 90-day plan):* Is this the kind of advice you could actually act on? What would make it more or less useful?
21. *(Show the simulator):* Is there anything here that would change your behavior before applying?

---

## Closing (5 min)

22. Is there anything about the loan application or rejection experience I haven't asked about that you think I should know?
23. If you could change one thing about how lenders communicate rejections, what would it be?

*"Thank you so much. Your answers were genuinely useful. I won't publish any names or identifying details."*

---

## Things I kept reminding myself

- Let them tell the story first, don't jump in with "did you feel X?" — that just confirms what you expect
- Never say "FOIR" until Q18 — I wanted to see if they'd bring up the concept themselves (most didn't know the word)
- Don't describe what I was building until Section 5 — I didn't want their reactions to be about my product, I wanted them to describe their actual experience first
- Write up key points same day while it's fresh
